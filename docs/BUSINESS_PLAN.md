## 1. Executive Summary

RELICK is a market intelligence platform that connects historical world events to market movements using AI. The product answers a question no platform answers well today: *Why did markets move the way they did — and what does history suggest comes next?*

The platform operates on two layers:
- **Free / Explorer:** Interactive market history education. AI-annotated price charts that explain the narrative behind every major move. A highlight-to-learn engine that explains any financial concept in plain English.
- **Pro / Intelligence:** A parallel-matching engine that accepts a current headline or thesis, searches the structured event knowledge graph for the closest historical analogs, ranks them by confidence score, and returns a professional analyst-grade memo with asset class implications.

The business model is a three-tier SaaS subscription: Explorer (free), Analyst ($29/month), Oracle ($99/month).

**The market opportunity:** Investment research platforms are a $7.5B market growing at 15.2% CAGR. AI-focused fintech attracted $1.52B in funding in 2024 alone. No dominant platform serves the $29–$99/month intelligent serious retail investor with event-driven, historically grounded market intelligence.

**The moat:** A structured, credibility-verified event knowledge graph that compounds in value daily. The longer it runs, the less replicable it becomes. This is RELICK's primary durable competitive advantage.

**Target acquirers:** Bloomberg (~$990M in data acquisitions), LSEG/Refinitiv ($27B Reuters deal), S&P Global ($550M Kensho), BlackRock (Aladdin).

## 2. Problem Statement

### The "What, Not Why" Problem

Every financial data platform tells investors *what* markets did. Bloomberg shows price. TradingView shows charts. Koyfin shows fundamentals. Hedgeye tells you what macro regime you’re in.

**Nobody tells you WHY.**

Why did the S&P drop 34% in 33 days in February–March 2020? A retail investor looking at TradingView sees a line chart. They have no structured context — no verified event annotation, no explanation of the feedback loops between fear, forced selling, Fed response, and market bottom.

### The Parallel Blindness Problem

Professional investors — hedge funds, macro traders, experienced portfolio managers — have institutional memory. When regional banks started collapsing in March 2023, experienced investors immediately pattern-matched to 2008, to the S&L crisis of the 1980s, to 1998 LTCM. They knew what to watch, what came next, what the Fed historically does.

The serious retail investor managing $200K in their IRA had no tool to access this pattern recognition. Twitter was noise. Reddit was speculation. Bloomberg was $32,000/year.

**The democratization gap:** The intelligence that Bloomberg Terminal users and Bridgewater researchers have always had — structured historical context, pattern recognition, event-to-market causal chains — is simply not available to anyone who can’t afford institutional access.

### The Verification Problem

AI tools for investors exist. ChatGPT, Perplexity, financial AI chatbots — users can ask "what happened in 2008?" and get a plausible-sounding answer. But:
- Sources are not verified
- Confidence scores don't exist
- Hallucination rates are material
- No structured, compounding knowledge graph underlies the response

Unverified AI in financial contexts is not just unhelpful. It can be harmful. RELICK’s 5-tier credibility scoring system and minimum 2-source verification rule are not just features — they are the foundation of institutional trust.

## 3. Solution & Product Overview

### What RELICK Is

RELICK is a dual-layer platform:

**Layer 1 — Free Education Engine (Explorer)**  
Interactive historical market charts with AI-generated event annotations. Users can explore any stock, index, or macro instrument across time, and at every major inflection point, RELICK explains what was happening in the world and why markets responded the way they did.

The **Highlight-to-Learn Engine** allows users to highlight any term in any annotation — "yield curve inversion," "quantitative tightening," "credit spread widening" — and instantly get a plain-English explanation of the mechanism and its historical market effects.

**Layer 2 — Intelligence Engine (Analyst & Oracle)**  
Users submit a current headline or thesis (e.g., "The Fed is signaling a pause while inflation remains above 3%"). The system:
1. Extracts quantitative and qualitative signals from the input
2. Searches the event knowledge graph using dual-signal matching (quant + qualitative)
3. Returns the top 3 historical parallels, ranked by confidence score
4. Generates a professional analyst memo: context, what happened to equities/bonds/FX/commodities, time horizon, risks to the thesis

**Oracle tier** adds live parallel monitoring — proactive alerts when live market conditions match a high-confidence historical pattern — plus community consensus features.

### The Name & Brand

**RELICK** carries three simultaneous meanings:
- **RELIC:** An ancient artifact that survived time and carries history forward
- **RE-LICK:** Sensory memory — taste something and it pulls you back to an exact moment. That involuntary recognition IS the parallel engine.
- **RELICK (verb):** "Did you RELICK the 2008 setup against what’s happening with regional banks right now?"

**Tagline:** *Re-taste history. See what’s coming.*

**Brand ethos:** Ancient authority meets radical democratization. The intelligence Bloomberg and Bridgewater have always had. Now for everyone.

**Animal spirit:** The elephant. Never forgets. Communicates through infrasound others can’t detect. The matriarch leads because her memory is the herd’s most valuable asset.

## 5. Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Waitlist & Community (Months 1–3)
**Objective:** Build pre-launch audience of 5,000–10,000 qualified prospects before product ships.

- Launch waitlist landing page with "Re-taste history. See what’s coming." positioning
- Publish 1–2 deep historical market stories per week on Substack/LinkedIn/X: *"What actually happened on Black Monday 1987"*, *"The 1998 LTCM crisis: every step, every market move"*
- Build Twitter/X presence around the daily "Today in Market History" format
- Seed in r/investing, r/SecurityAnalysis, r/MacroEconomics with genuine educational content
- Target: 5,000 waitlist subscribers in 90 days

### Phase 2: Closed Beta (Months 4–6)
**Objective:** Validate core product, gather NPS data, fix UX issues, generate case studies.

- Invite 500 waitlist subscribers (select for engagement quality)
- Focus on the Submit Intel workflow end-to-end: input quality, output quality, memo usefulness
- NPS target: 50+
- Testimonial capture: 10+ compelling quotes from beta users
- Refine the highlight-to-learn engine based on which terms users highlight most

### Phase 3: Public Launch (Month 7)
**Objective:** Generate 500–1,000 paid subscribers in launch month.

**Launch week playbook:**
- Product Hunt launch — aim for #1 Product of the Day (drives 2,000–5,000 signups)
- 3–5 financial influencer partnerships activated simultaneously (each sends to 50K–200K subscribers)
- Press: TechCrunch, The Information, Bloomberg (yes — they’ll cover a product that competes with their data layer)
- Founder AMA on r/investing
- Launch pricing: first 500 Analyst subscribers locked at $19/month for life (converts urgency)

### Phase 4: Scale (Months 8–18)
**Objective:** Hit $100K MRR within 12 months of launch.

- Content engine: daily "Today in Market History" is consistent social content
- SEO: build out the historical event content library for long-tail organic acquisition
- Newsletter sponsorships: rotate through 5–8 finance newsletters with 50K+ subscribers
- Referral program: Analyst subscribers get 1 free month per referral who converts
- Oracle conversion: proactively identify Analyst subscribers with high engagement; offer trial Oracle features for 7 days free
